2nd Year, 1st Semester - Defqon Wristband

Design Brief

For a museum, theatre or festival in Sydney design a digital/tangible/spatial experience that builds upon the content they offer (e.g. performances, collections). It should improve the visitor experience and help the institution to engage with new audiences, or affirm their relationship with existing ones. The experience should be closely interwoven with the context; it could not exist without that environment.

Research Methods

Context-mapping sessions allow researchers to access a participant’s latent knowledge through the creation of collages and mind maps. The collages are from a pool of ambiguous images and words. After making their collages, participants share what they’ve made and are interviewed on their process.

Extension of Design Brief

We then had to decide what direction we wanted to go into based on our research insights.
We decided to focus on the drug aspect as it was topical at the time with the imminent shutdown of music festivals.
It was an interesting problem to work on because the government’s approach to increase security presence actually encouraged drug users to adopt more risky and opportunistic behaviour.

Final design

After a process of ideation and iteration, we decided on a final design taking on the form of a wristband.

The Defqon Wristband is a wearable that takes inspiration from multiple existing technologies and combines them to create a specialised experience for Defqon attendees with a focus on keeping illicit substance users safe. It is the result of research into wearable technology and biometrics, the utilisation of a decision matrix as well as expert feedback. With features aimed to address context specific problems while building upon the existing content of the festival, the final design is directly tailored toward the initial design brief and issues revealed through user research.
